🏗️ Construction & Flooring

Sourcing smarter for the construction industry.

The construction and flooring industry is one of On3 Link's most active sourcing categories. From tile leveling clips and spacers to flooring accessories, building materials, and installation tools — we source products that contractors, distributors, and retailers need to run their businesses.

Our OBP USA division operates directly in this space, importing tile leveling systems and flooring accessories for distribution across the US market. On3 Link sources consumer products across personal care, home goods, lifestyle accessories, outdoor products, and general merchandise — with a supply base concentrated in China's manufacturing heartland, supported by Brazilian and Mexican manufacturers where quality or proximity advantages exist.

🛒 Retail & Wholesale
🛒

Retail success starts with the right supply chain.

Retail buyers and wholesale distributors operate in one of the most demanding supply chain environments in business. Margins are thin, customer expectations are high, inventory timing is critical, and a supply chain failure can mean empty shelves — or worse, an over-committed inventory position with a supplier who can't deliver.

On3 Link works with retail and wholesale businesses to build sourcing strategies that prioritize reliability over lowest price. Because in retail, a missed delivery or a quality failure costs more than the savings from choosing the cheapest supplier.

We identify suppliers with proven export track records, consistent production capacity, and the quality management systems required to support a retail supply chain — then develop those supplier relationships so they perform over time, not just on the first order.

"Retail buyers don't need the cheapest supplier. They need the most reliable one at a competitive price. That distinction shapes every sourcing recommendation we make in this category."

What We Provide for Retail & Wholesale

  • Supplier identification and vetting for retail-grade quality
  • Landed cost analysis to protect retail margins
  • Minimum order quantity negotiation

🏭 Manufacturing
🏭

Manufacturers need better supply chains too.

It's easy to think of international sourcing as something retailers and importers do. But manufacturers are among the most active users of international supply chains — sourcing components, raw materials, sub-assemblies, and packaging from overseas suppliers to reduce production costs and maintain competitive pricing.

For US manufacturers, the combination of rising domestic input costs and increasing global competition makes international sourcing not just an option — but a strategic necessity. The question is not whether to source internationally, but how to do it in a way that doesn't introduce unacceptable quality or supply chain risk.

On3 Link works with manufacturing businesses to identify and develop reliable overseas component and material suppliers — with a particular focus on Mexico's nearshoring opportunity, which allows US manufacturers to access lower-cost production with dramatically reduced supply chain complexity compared to Asia.

"Manufacturing businesses need supply chains as reliable as their own production lines. We build supplier relationships that can handle production schedules — not just one-off orders."

What We Source for Manufacturers

  • Components and sub-assemblies
  • Raw materials and inputs
  • Packaging and labeling materials
  • Tooling and molds for OEM production
  • Industrial equipment and machinery
  • Contract manufacturing and assembly

Mexico Nearshoring for Manufacturers

For US manufacturers, Mexico represents a compelling nearshoring opportunity — lower labor costs, USMCA trade benefits, and 2–5 day transit times instead of 30+ days from Asia. On3 Link has active supplier relationships in Mexico's key manufacturing corridors.
