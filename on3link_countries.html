Brazil is not the first country most US buyers think of for sourcing — and that's exactly what makes it a strategic advantage for On3 Link clients. While competitors flood Chinese factories with RFQs, our clients access Brazilian manufacturers with excess capacity, high-quality output, and genuine interest in US export business.

Our Brazilian network is particularly strong in the professional barber and salon equipment category — where Brazilian manufacturers like APF Medical produce barber chairs and salon furniture that rival European quality at significantly lower landed costs.

Brazil's manufacturing capabilities extend across commercial furniture, industrial products, flooring and construction materials, personal care equipment, and specialized consumer goods. For businesses seeking quality differentiation and supplier diversity, Brazil delivers.

Importing from Brazil does require experience with Brazilian export regulations and documentation — an area where On3 Link's on-the-ground knowledge becomes essential.

"Brazil offers product quality and manufacturing capability that most US importers have never explored. Our clients who source from Brazil often find their best-performing products."

China is On3 Link's deepest sourcing market. Our network spans manufacturing hubs across Guangdong, Zhejiang, Jiangsu, and Fujian provinces — covering virtually every product category relevant to US importers.

What separates On3 Link from a simple sourcing agent is how we build Chinese supplier relationships. We attend the Canton Fair in Guangzhou — one of the world's largest trade exhibitions, held twice yearly — where we meet manufacturers face-to-face, inspect products in person, and build the kind of relationships that produce reliable long-term supply.

For OEM manufacturing, we connect you with factories capable of building to your exact specifications — managing tooling, sampling, production oversight, and quality control through the entire development process.

For businesses concerned about China supply chain risk, we provide a balanced perspective: China's manufacturing depth and cost structure remain unmatched for many product categories, and with the right supplier relationships and quality controls in place, the risks are manageable.

"The difference between a good China sourcing experience and a bad one is almost always the quality of the supplier relationship — and that's built in person, not online."

Mexico

Nearshoring for the
North American supply chain.

Mexico has emerged as the fastest-growing nearshoring destination for US manufacturers and importers. With USMCA trade benefits, dramatically shorter supply chains, and a rapidly expanding manufacturing base, Mexico offers a compelling alternative — and complement — to Asian sourcing.

🏗️

Nearshoring

Move production closer to the US to reduce lead times and supply chain risk

📜

USMCA Benefits

Take advantage of preferential trade terms under the US-Mexico-Canada Agreement

⚡

Shorter Lead Times

Days to the US border instead of weeks across the Pacific

🔗

Supply Diversification

Reduce China dependency while maintaining competitive cost structures

The geopolitical shift away from sole reliance on Chinese manufacturing has accelerated Mexico's emergence as a major manufacturing destination. Nearshoring — moving production to a nearby country to reduce supply chain complexity — has made Mexico one of the most strategically important markets of the decade for US businesses.

Mexico's manufacturing capabilities have expanded significantly across automotive, electronics assembly, consumer goods, packaging, medical devices, and more. The combination of lower labor costs, geographic proximity to the US, and USMCA preferential trade terms creates a powerful value proposition.

On3 Link is actively building supplier relationships in Mexico's key manufacturing corridors — including Monterrey, Guadalajara, Juárez, and the Bajío region — positioning our clients to take advantage of nearshoring opportunities before they become mainstream and competitive.

For businesses currently sourcing entirely from China, Mexico offers a diversification path that maintains cost competitiveness while dramatically reducing supply chain risk and lead times.

"Mexico's moment is now. The businesses building supplier relationships there today will have a strategic advantage that competitors will spend years trying to replicate."

Why Nearshore to Mexico?

  • 2–5 day transit vs. 30+ days from Asia
  • USMCA preferential tariff treatment
  • Lower supply chain disruption risk
  • Easier quality control and factory visits
  • Shared time zones for communication
  • Growing manufacturing infrastructure

Key Manufacturing Regions

  • Monterrey — Industrial, metals, auto parts
  • Guadalajara — Electronics, tech assembly
  • Ciudad Juárez — Cross-border manufacturing
  • Bajío Region — Auto, aerospace, consumer goods
  • Tijuana — Medical devices, electronics

What On3 Provides in Mexico

  • Nearshoring feasibility assessment
  • Mexican supplier identification and vetting
  • Spanish-language negotiation support
  • USMCA compliance guidance
  • Cross-border logistics coordination
  • Factory visit and audit support
