01 — International Product Sourcing
01

Finding the right manufacturer changes everything.

Most businesses approach sourcing like a search engine query — type in a product, scroll through results, pick the cheapest option. That approach fails more often than it succeeds.

At On3 Link, international product sourcing starts with understanding your business. Your quality standards, your target market, your price point, your growth plans. Only then do we begin identifying manufacturers who can actually deliver — not just promise.

Our sourcing network spans China, Brazil, Mexico, and the United States. We tap into relationships built face-to-face at trade shows, factory floors, and supplier meetings — not databases and directories.

02 — Supplier Development
02

We don't find suppliers. We develop them.

Finding a factory is easy. Building a reliable supplier relationship is not. Most sourcing problems don't happen because the factory was hard to find — they happen because the relationship was never properly developed.

Supplier development is the process of turning a potential manufacturer into a trusted, long-term partner. It involves auditing their operations, verifying their certifications, testing their communication, aligning on quality expectations, and — critically — building the kind of relationship where problems get solved before they ship.

This is where On3 Link invests more time than any other step. Because a well-developed supplier relationship is the foundation everything else is built on.

What's Included

  • Factory audit and capability assessment
  • Certification and compliance verification
  • Quality system evaluation
  • Communication and responsiveness testing
  • On-site visits and face-to-face meetings
  • Long-term relationship management
  • Supplier performance tracking

Why it matters

80% of sourcing failures trace back to a supplier that was never properly vetted. Supplier development is the step most companies skip — and the one that costs them the most.

What we evaluate

  • Factory capacity and production capability
  • Quality control processes
  • Export experience and documentation
  • Financial stability indicators
  • Communication quality and speed

03 — Strategic Procurement
03

Better purchasing decisions start with better information.

Procurement is more than placing purchase orders. Strategic procurement means understanding total landed costs, negotiating payment terms that protect your cash flow, locking in quality specifications before production starts, and building contingency into your supply timeline.

Most companies negotiate on price alone. We negotiate on total value — price, quality, lead time, payment terms, minimum order quantities, packaging, and long-term partnership conditions.

When you work with On3 Link on procurement, you're not just getting a lower price. You're getting a better deal — one that holds up three, six, and twelve months down the line.

What's Included

  • Total landed cost analysis (product + freight + duties + fees)
  • Supplier negotiation on price, terms, and MOQ
  • Purchase order management and tracking
  • Payment terms strategy (T/T, LC, escrow)
  • Quality specification documentation
  • Pre-production and pre-shipment inspection coordination
  • Dispute resolution support

The On3 Approach to Negotiation

We negotiate from knowledge, not guesswork. Our market intelligence across four countries means we know what a fair price looks like — and we won't let you overpay.

What we negotiate

  • Unit price and volume discounts
  • Minimum order quantities (MOQ)
  • Payment terms and conditions
  • Lead times and production scheduling
  • Packaging and labeling requirements
  • Warranty and return policies
04 — OEM & ODM Manufacturing
04

Your product. Built by the right factory.

OEM (Original Equipment Manufacturing) means the factory builds your product to your exact specifications. You own the design. They manufacture it. This is the path for companies with a clear product vision who need a production partner.

ODM (Original Design Manufacturing) means the factory has an existing product design that you can brand and modify. This is faster and less expensive — ideal for businesses launching new product lines without the time or budget for full product development.

On3 Link connects you with OEM and ODM manufacturers across our network — particularly in China, where our Canton Fair relationships provide access to factories across virtually every product category.

What's Included

  • OEM vs. ODM strategy consultation
  • Factory identification and capability matching
  • Product specification documentation
  • Sample development and revision management
  • Tooling and mold coordination
  • Production oversight and quality control
  • IP and confidentiality agreement guidance

OEM vs. ODM — Which is right for you?

  • OEM — You have a design, need a builder
  • ODM — You need a product, want speed to market
  • Hybrid — Start ODM, evolve to OEM over time

Industries we serve for OEM/ODM

  • Consumer Electronics & Accessories
  • Construction & Building Materials
  • Home Improvement Products
  • Automotive Accessories
  • Industrial & Commercial Equipment
05 — Private Label Development
05

Build your brand on world-class manufacturing.

Private label is one of the most powerful strategies available to growing businesses. You take a quality-manufactured product, brand it as your own, and sell it at a margin that reflects your brand's value — not just the factory's cost.

The challenge? Doing it right requires finding the right manufacturer, developing the right packaging, meeting the right compliance requirements, and building a supply chain reliable enough to support your brand promise.

On3 Link manages the entire private label process — from identifying the right factory to delivering branded, shelf-ready product. We've done this across beauty, barber, construction, consumer goods, and more.

What's Included

  • Product category research and factory selection
  • Private label manufacturer vetting and validation
  • Branding and packaging coordination
  • Label compliance review (FDA, FCC, CE, etc.)
  • Sample development and approval
  • Production and quality management
  • Shipping and import coordination

Why Private Label?

  • Higher margins than reselling branded products
  • Full control over branding and positioning
  • Builds long-term brand equity
  • Differentiates you from competitors
  • Scalable as your business grows

Categories We Support

06 — Import & Export Support
06

International trade is complex. We simplify it.

Import and export operations involve a maze of documentation, compliance requirements, shipping logistics, customs procedures, and regulatory frameworks that vary by country, product category, and incoterm. One missed document can hold a container at port for weeks.

On3 Link provides import and export coordination support across all four countries in our network. We work alongside your customs broker and freight forwarder to ensure every document is correct, every requirement is met, and every shipment moves efficiently.

We don't replace your logistics team — we make them more effective by ensuring the sourcing and production side of the supply chain feeds clean, complete information into the logistics chain.

What's Included

  • Import and export documentation review
  • Incoterms strategy and selection
  • HS code classification support
  • Duty and tariff analysis
  • Compliance requirement identification by country
  • Freight forwarder coordination
  • Shipping timeline management

Countries We Support

  • 🇺🇸 USA imports and exports
  • 🇧🇷 Brazil — complex import regulations
  • 🇨🇳 China — factory to port documentation
  • 🇲🇽 Mexico — USMCA and cross-border trade

Key Incoterms We Work With

  • CIF — Cost, Insurance, Freight
  • EXW — Ex Works
  • DDP — Delivered Duty Paid
  • DAP — Delivered At Place
07 — Supply Chain Solutions
07

Build a supply chain your business can depend on.

A supply chain is only as strong as its weakest link. And for many businesses, that weak link is over-dependence on a single supplier, a single country, or a single route to market.

Supply chain disruptions — whether from geopolitical events, factory shutdowns, quality failures, or shipping crises — can halt a business overnight. The companies that survive these disruptions are the ones that built resilience into their supply chain before the crisis hit.

On3 Link helps businesses build diversified, resilient supply chains across our four-country network. Whether you're moving production closer to North America through nearshoring in Mexico, diversifying China risk into Brazil, or building a multi-supplier strategy, we design and implement the solution.

What's Included

  • Supply chain audit and vulnerability assessment
  • Supplier diversification strategy
  • Nearshoring evaluation (Mexico focus)
  • Multi-source supplier development
  • Inventory and lead time optimization
  • Risk mitigation planning
  • Ongoing supply chain monitoring

Nearshoring to Mexico

With the rise of USMCA and the push to shorten supply chains, Mexico has become one of the most strategic manufacturing destinations for US businesses. On3 Link has active relationships in key Mexican manufacturing corridors.

Supply Chain Risks We Address

  • Single-supplier dependency
  • Geographic concentration risk
  • Long lead times and inventory exposure
  • Currency and tariff exposure
  • Quality consistency challenges
08 — Trade Show Representation
08

We attend trade shows so you don't have to travel internationally.

The world's best supplier relationships are still built face-to-face. International trade shows like the Canton Fair in Guangzhou, China are where manufacturers showcase their latest products, meet new customers, and negotiate deals that rarely happen any other way.

The problem? Getting to these shows requires international travel, language capability, industry knowledge, and the experience to navigate thousands of booths and identify the right manufacturers. Most businesses simply don't have the resources to do this effectively.

On3 Link attends these events on your behalf. We walk the floors, meet the manufacturers, collect samples, attend supplier meetings, and bring back actionable intelligence about your market — all without you needing to board a plane.
